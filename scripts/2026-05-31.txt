Parashah: Beha'alotcha (Numbers 8:1-12:16)
Date: 2026-05-31

Welcome to Parashah in Brief! I'm so glad you're here with me this week. We're diving into Parashat Beha'alotcha, which comes from the book of Numbers and picks up the story of the Israelites as they prepare to leave Mount Sinai and begin their long journey through the wilderness toward the Promised Land.

<break time="1.5s" />

Here is our summary of Numbers, chapter eight, verse one, through chapter twelve, verse sixteen. The portion opens with God instructing Aaron to light the seven lamps of the golden menorah, the sacred lampstand, in the Tabernacle. Then the Levites — the tribe set apart for holy service — are formally purified and dedicated to serve in the Tent of Meeting, taking the place of every firstborn son in Israel. Next, the people celebrate the Passover sacrifice in the wilderness, and when some men who are ritually impure ask Moses what to do, God provides a make-up date for them one month later — a beautiful moment of inclusion. We then learn how the cloud over the Tabernacle guided the Israelites' every move: when it lifted, they marched; when it settled, they camped — whether for a day, a month, or a year. Two silver trumpets are crafted to signal the community when to assemble and when to march, and finally the great departure from Sinai begins, tribe by tribe, in careful order. But almost immediately, trouble starts. The people begin complaining, and fire from God burns at the edges of the camp until Moses intercedes. Then the people weep for meat, reminiscing about the fish, cucumbers, and garlic of Egypt and grumbling about the manna. Moses himself is overwhelmed and cries out to God, "I cannot carry all this people by myself!" God responds by placing His spirit on seventy elders to share the burden of leadership, and then sends a massive wind that sweeps quail from the sea — but those who gorge themselves are struck with a deadly plague. The portion closes with Miriam and Aaron challenging Moses' unique authority, and Miriam is stricken with a skin disease. Moses tenderly prays for her healing, and she is restored after seven days outside the camp.
