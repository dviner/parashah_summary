Parashah: Tzav (Leviticus 6:1-8:36)
Date: 2026-03-22

Welcome to Parashah in Brief, so glad you're here with me this week. Today we're looking at Parashat Tzav, which means "Command," and it's the second portion in the book of Leviticus. Last week we got our first introduction to the sacrificial system at the newly built Tabernacle, and now God goes deeper, giving Moses detailed instructions for the priests who will carry out this sacred work.

<break time="1.5s" />

Here is our summary of Leviticus, chapter six, verse one, through chapter eight, verse thirty-six. God speaks to Moses and tells him to command Aaron and his sons — the newly appointed priests — in the precise rituals they must follow. First comes the burnt offering, which must stay on the altar all night long while a perpetual fire burns, a fire that must never go out. The priest must dress in linen garments, remove the ashes each morning, change his clothes, and carry the ashes outside the camp to a ritually pure place. Then God lays out the rules for the grain offering, the purgation offering — which addresses sin — and the reparation offering, which deals with guilt. Each one has its own careful procedure: what gets burned, what the priests may eat, where they must eat it, and what vessels can be used. God also gives instructions for the well-being offering, a sacrifice of thanksgiving and fellowship, along with strict warnings — no one may eat the fat or the blood of an animal, and anyone who eats sacred meat while in a state of impurity will be cut off from the community. Then comes the dramatic climax of the portion. God tells Moses to gather the entire community at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting for the ordination of Aaron and his sons. Moses washes them, dresses Aaron in the magnificent priestly garments — the tunic, the robe, the breastpiece, and the golden headpiece — and anoints him and the Tabernacle with sacred oil. One by one, three offerings are brought: a bull for purification, a ram for a burnt offering, and a second ram of ordination, whose blood Moses dabs on Aaron's right ear, right thumb, and right big toe, and then on his sons in the same way. Moses places portions of the offerings into the hands of Aaron and his sons and they elevate them before God. Finally, Moses instructs them to remain at the entrance of the Tent of Meeting for seven full days to complete their consecration — and Aaron and his sons faithfully do everything that God commanded.
